The Neuroscience of Marketing


Neuroeconomics describes how incentives are created and analyzed in order to determine behavior. This is the discipline which defines how economic incentives and rewards interact with people. If you are a computer scientist, a general manager, a hard worker, or even a college dropout, then you probably have some appreciation for the idea of creating incentives to get people to do things.

If you believe in the work of Thorstein Veblen, George Lipsitz, and similar thinkers, you probably also recognize that marketing and advertising have a significant influence on behavior.
